Selectors Challenge


class="salim">"Learning CSS selectors is like unlocking the hidden power of web design. First, element selectors let you style all <p> or <h1> tags in a page at once. Second, class selectors allow you to target groups of elements that share the same purpose, such as buttons or cards. Third, ID selectors focus on a single unique element, perfect for headers or special sections. Fourth, combinators help you style elements based on their relationships, like parent > child or sibling + sibling. Fifth, pseudo-classes let you add effects like hover, focus, or active states to make interactions dynamic. Sixth, pseudo-elements like ::first-letter or ::before add creative touches to text or content. Seventh, attribute selectors can target elements with specific properties, such as inputs with a certain type or links with a specific href. Finally, understanding specificity ensures your styles are applied exactly where you want them, avoiding conflicts and making your designs clean and professional."
